What does it do?

The Predictor scores every horse in a race and ranks them in order of predicted finishing position, drawing on a model trained on years of UK and Irish flat turf results.

🐎 The horse

Win rate, going and distance preference, course record.

🧑 Jockey & trainer

Individual win rates from thousands of rides.

📊 Market odds

SP is a strong signal — bookmakers have enormous data.

📅 Freshness

How recently the horse last ran. Long breaks often need the run.

⚠️
This is a tool, not a guarantee. Racing is unpredictable. Use it to inform your thinking, not replace it. Gamble responsibly.
2
First-time setup

The app needs a racing_model.json file to work at full power — your host will have sent this to you.

  1. Save the racing_model.json file to your phone (Downloads or Files app).
  2. Tap the Setup tab, then Tap to load racing_model.json and select the file.
  3. A green confirmation appears when loaded. Done — it stays active for this session.
ℹ️
No model? Tap Activate Default Weights in Setup. Results are less personalised but still useful.
3
Making a prediction

The app reads race data from a PDF form guide downloaded free from attheraces.com.

  1. Go to attheraces.com, find your race, and download the form guide PDF.
  2. Tap the Races tab, then load the PDF. All runners are extracted automatically.
  3. Tap the race you want to predict, then tap Predict.
  4. Enter decimal odds for each runner (see tip below), set the going, then tap Predict This Race.
💰
Decimal odds: Convert fractional to decimal by dividing top by bottom and adding 1. So 7/2 = (7÷2)+1 = 4.5. Evens = 2.0. Most bookmaker apps have a decimal display option.
💡
Enter odds as close to race time as possible — the final market is far more informative than early prices. Aim for within 30 minutes of the off.
4
Setting the going

Always check the official going report on race day — racingpost.com or the racecourse website usually has it. Going can change between morning and race time.

FirmVery fast, hard ground. Rare on British turf.

Good To SoftSome give underfoot. After light rain. Favours stayers.

7
Common questions
❓ PDF loaded but no runners appeared
The app reads At The Races form guide PDFs only. PDFs from Racing Post, racecourses or bookmakers use different formats and may not parse correctly.
❓ All horses show the same score
Odds haven't been entered yet — they're a big part of the scoring. Enter decimal odds for each runner and the scores will spread out.
❓ Does it work for jump racing?
The model was trained on UK and Irish flat turf only. It can still rank jump race runners but accuracy will be lower — jump racing has very different dynamics.
❓ Should I always back the predicted winner?
No. Use it to identify horses worth looking at more closely, then apply your own judgement. Blindly following any system is a reliable way to lose money over time.
❓ What's the model's win rate?
You can see the test accuracy in the Setup tab once the model is loaded. As a reference, picking the winner by chance from a field of 12 would be right about 8% of the time — a well-calibrated model does meaningfully better, though no model wins consistently.
When the fun stops, stop.  ·  GambleAware.org
